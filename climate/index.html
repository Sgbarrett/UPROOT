CLIMATE CHANGE IS REAL AND YOU CAN HELP

Our Carbon Footprint

It is common knowledge that many of the daily things we do can have an impact on the world around us. Everything from driving to work, buying takeout often, buying seasonal clothes, and leaving the sink running while you brush your teeth leaves a mark on our planet. Your carbon footprint is the level of carbon compounds emitted due to your everyday life and activities. While there is no way of getting around carbon emissions, we all can do our part as individuals to reduce waste and shrink our carbon footprint.

How Buying Food Impacts the Environment

Many of us are already trying to change our habits to help our planet, and that is great! We carpool, use cloth bags at the grocery store, recycle our plastics, and donate our clothes. While all of this is important work, did you know that the Climate Action Business Association reported that 8% of our personal carbon footprint comes from food alone? That food, produced on farms and in factories, then has to be shipped all over the nation and distributed to your local grocery stores to finally be purchased by you and end up at its final resting place: your dining room table.

What We Can Do to Reduce Our Footprint

We need to eat to survive, so some may see the impact of the food industry to be a necessary evil. While this is mostly true, there are things we can do as individuals to continue to lesson this issue. Buying locally grown food is a big part of it. Local food means less fuel used to get the product to your home. Another great thing to try is growing your own food! Even if it starts as a small pepper plant or some herbs, gardening is a hobby that is beneficial for you and the planet. Composting is extremely beneficial, leading to lowered carbon emissions from landfills and healthy plants.
