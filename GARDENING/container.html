Container Gardening

A perfect solution for urban gardening.


Indoor vs Outdoor Container Gardening

Indoor Container Gardening is accessible for nearly any individual as long as sunlight, water, and nutrients necessary for growth can be provided. Outdoor container gardening eliminates the previous concerns, although its success is more influenced by the outdoor climate and its positioning to access light, nutrients, and water.


Container Sizes and Materials

Containers can be made from many materials such as clay, plastic, metal, wood, concrete, and much more.

  • 1 pint containers are best suited for seedlings
  • 1 - 3 quarts containers are best suited for some seedlings, but mostly for succulents and transplants
  • 1- 2 gallons containers are best suited for small herbs and vegetables, as well as typical houseplants
  • 3-5 gallons containers are best suited for most vegetables and small fruits like cantaloupe

Preparation and Care

When using a plant pot,be sure to place it in the area you wish for them to go as it may be too heavy to move after filling it. Place a cloth, coffee filter, or old old shards at the bottom of the bottom so the potting mix will not fall out, but allow water drainage Be sure to have plenty of potting mix and fertilizer. Do not mix soil from the outside with the mix as it may be filled with pests.



Watering and Drainage

Be sure when watering that you pour enough water where you can see it is draining out. This is to ensure the water is reaching to the bottom of the roots as we are watering the soil, not the plant.
