Hydroponics

An excellent way to grow plants inside



Why Use a Hydroponics System?

Hydroponics is best suited for areas where outdoor space is limited or not available and/or water must be conserved. Hydroponic gardening uses 70% less water than conventional methods of gardening, reducing costs for gardeners and water systems. This method is best practiced indoors to isolate the climate and pests from the plant. In addition, the gardener can provide constant access to light to promote plant maturation, reducing time for harvest.

Starting a Hydroponic Garden

  1. Research and learn about the Top drip system
  2. Pick your plant
  3. Prepare a water reservoir or water source
  4. Define and select your light source
  5. Purchase a pH monitor
  6. Prepare a growing bed
  7. Choose a nutrient solution
  8. Provide a support base for your plants
