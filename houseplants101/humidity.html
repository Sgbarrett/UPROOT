Humidity

An essential factor in creating the best environment possible for your plants to thrive



Picky Plants

Another important factor in choosing a plant is the humidity in your home. A number of common houseplants come from the steamy rainforests of Central and South America. While many will adapt to the humidity in our homes without much trouble, some plants are a bit pickier. Prayer plants, for example, may get dry edges on their leaves if the humidity is too low. These pretty plants do their best when humidity is consistently above around 40 to 50 percent.


Testing your homes humidity

You can measure the humidity in your home with a - you guessed it - humidity sensor. There are, of course, dedicated devices for doing so, but you can also find them in inexpensive clocks that also tell temperature, and even some smartphones. Take the readings with a grain of salt, but it should give you a ballpark estimate to work with.


Humidity Solutions

If your home is bone-dry and you have good lighting, consider arid climate adapted species of cacti and succulents. A humidifier can help keep your space livable for more tropical species. You can also make a pebble tray. Simply fill a saucer with stones, add water, but not to the top, and set your plants on the stones. Make sure the plants aren’t sitting in water. The water in the tray will evaporate and help add humidity around the plants.


Misting

Misting your plants is often recommended to help with humidity, but please don’t do this. Unless you have very good airflow, the water droplets will just sit on the leaves and can encourage fungal issues. In a sunny window, the droplets can magnify the sun’s rays and scorch the leaves. Misting doesn’t even help that much. It barely increases humidity, and only for a short time. All around, not worth it!


Steamy rooms

If your bathroom has good lighting, consider keeping your pickier plants in there. They’ll really appreciate the steam from the shower.
